Enjoy a Nomad Adventure Eagle Hunting!



Step into the ancient world of Mongolian nomads and experience one of the world's oldest hunting traditions ‚Äî eagle hunting with the Kazakh people of Western Mongolia. This immersive expedition invites you to live alongside real herding families, ride through breathtaking landscapes on horseback, and witness the extraordinary bond between a hunter and a golden eagle.

Tour Overview:

This 8-day cultural adventure takes place mainly in the Altai Mountains of Bayan-√ñlgii Province, home to Mongolia's ethnic Kazakh community. The region is famous for its snow-capped peaks, glacier valleys and the deep-rooted art of berkutchi ‚Äî hunting foxes and hares with trained female golden eagles. Guests will stay with local nomadic families, learning about their seasonal migrations, livestock care, traditional Kazakh crafts and the delicate training of the golden eagle.

Highlights:

Live in a traditional Kazakh ger surrounded by the towering Altai Mountains, where you will take part in the daily life of nomadic herders. Watch how golden eagles are fed, cared for and trained. Learn basic riding techniques from expert horsemen and follow a real hunting route with a local berkutchi. Guests may also participate in traditional Kazakh cooking, embroidery, throat singing and musical performances using instruments such as the dombra.

Best Time to Travel:

Eagle hunting season typically runs from October to March, when the cold weather enhances visibility and hunting conditions. The warmer months (June-September) are ideal for nomadic lifestyle immersion and mountain rides, though active hunting may not occur during summer.

Included in This Tour:

Accommodation in traditional gers, all meals during the nomadic stay, transport, horses, national park fees, eagle hunter fees and the service of experienced guides are included. Not included: international flights, personal insurance, and optional activities.

Safety & Cultural Notes:

The Altai region is remote and mountainous, and temperatures can drop very low during winter hunts. Guests should pack warm layers, waterproof boots and be prepared for rustic living. Respect for the eagle hunter and host family is essential; certain actions such as touching the eagle without permission are strictly prohibited.
